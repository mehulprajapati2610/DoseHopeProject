The process

How DoseHope Works

From your shelf to someone who needs it — in four tracked steps.

01

List your medicine

Add the name, batch number, manufacturing and expiry date, and a photo. We auto-check expiry so nothing unsafe gets listed.

02

Get matched

A verified NGO or hospital near you requests it. You review and accept — you stay in control of who receives your donation.

03

Volunteer pickup

A vetted volunteer collects the medicine from your doorstep and tracks the handoff at every stage.

04

Delivered & confirmed

The NGO confirms receipt. You get a notification and it's added to your donation history.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Is it safe to donate unused medicine?

Yes — DoseHope only accepts medicines that are unexpired, in original sealed packaging, and verified against batch and expiry details before they're listed.

Who picks up my donation?

A verified volunteer assigned through the platform handles pickup, so you don't have to coordinate delivery yourself.

How do I know my donation reached someone?

You'll get a notification at every step — accepted, picked up, and delivered — and it stays in your donation history for reference.

Can hospitals and NGOs request specific medicines?

Yes, verified NGOs and hospitals can browse available listings and send requests for medicines matching their current need.
